Japanese Weapons Render

Katana UV & Textures

Wakizashi UV & Texture

This case study explores the iconic weapons of Japanese martial culture - the Katana and Wakizashi. These weapons are not only symbols of Japan's historical warfare but also of the country's rich craftsmanship and tactical ingenuity.

The creation of both blades starts with accurately capturing its sleek curves, distinctive blade shape, and the fine details of the hilt and guard. A mid - high metallic textures are applied to the blade to mimic the traditional steel finish, while the wooden handle and metal guard are given realistic finishes like wood grain and polished metal.

Semi - Realistic Mewtwo

Mewtwo UV & Texture

Mewtwo Armor UV & Texture

Project explores the process of creating the iconic Mewtwo from Pokemon in a semi - realistic art style. First step was to blockout Mewtwo's unique anatomy, such as its elongated tail, head structure, and muscular build, requires careful attention to detail. Then I proceeded to sculpt in the details, defining the facial structure, skin texture and battle scars.

After finishing the sculpting phase, I moved on to retopology, practicing to optimize the wireframe for a game, so reducing as much of the wireframe while keeping the quality of the mesh, especially at the joints for animation.
For texturing the eyes are given a glossy, reflective texture to mimic the realistic shine of a living creature's eyes, while the rest of the face retains a more stylized smoothness. Rather than a flat, cartoony color, the skin hue is modeled with slight gradients and variations, which emulate how light interacts with skin in the real world. Scars are darken so is the armor mimic the dark steel woren down through battles.

Texture Study

First identify the metal parts of the model, the model's metal surfaces are given a rusted, worn appearance, applying a darkened base color map to give oxidized metal surface. Add layered rust through decals, grunge maps and quick usage of alphas on Zbrush to generate normal map with bumps in order to create corrosion effect. To add overall weathering and details to the model, I applied rust scratches, stains throughout to create an uneven effect around the model.
